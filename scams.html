Avoid Scams თაღლითობის თავიდან აცილება

თაღლითობის თავიდან აცილება · Éviter les arnaques

!

Newcomers are the #1 target. Scammers know you don't fully understand Belgian rules yet, that you trust other Georgians automatically, and that you may be desperate for housing or work. The patterns below repeat every year. Recognising them costs nothing.

ახლადჩამოსულები ყველაზე ხშირი სამიზნეა. თაღლითებმა იციან, რომ თქვენ ჯერ არ გესმით ბელგიის წესები და სასწრაფოდ გჭირდებათ საცხოვრებელი ან სამუშაო. ქვემოთ ჩამოთვლილი ნიმუშები ყოველწლიურად მეორდება.

Common scam patterns

თაღლითობის გავრცელებული ნიმუშები

#1

Apartment "deposit" before viewing

"Send €1,500 deposit to secure the flat — I'm in another city, my cousin will give you keys." Photos are real (stolen from Immoweb). Once you transfer, the "landlord" disappears.

→ Never pay before viewing in person. Demand the blocked-account procedure (huurwaarborg / garantie locative). Cash to a stranger = gone.

#2

"Fast track" residence permit

Someone claiming connections at the commune offers to "speed up" your residence card for €500–€2000 cash. The commune doesn't take cash and doesn't have a fast track. You lose the money.

→ All commune processes are free or have published fees. If someone asks for cash off-the-books, they're lying.

#3

Fake job offer with "registration fee"

"Construction job, €15/hour, but you need to pay €200 registration first." Real employers never charge candidates. Some demand a passport copy first, then sell the data.

→ No real job costs you money to start. Never send your full passport scan unless you've signed a contract.

#4

"I'll lend you money — sign here"

An "older Georgian" offers an informal loan with a contract you can't read in Dutch/French. The contract often hides 30–50% interest, or transfers your future wages directly to them.

→ Never sign a contract you can't read. Use a Belgian bank, a credit union, or licensed microcredit (e.g. Microstart). Get the contract translated by a sworn translator.

#5

Cash-paid work without contract

Worker arrives, does the job, employer pays in cash, no contract, no payslip. Then refuses to pay the last weeks. With no contract, you can't prove you ever worked there.

→ Always require a signed contract before starting. Cash-only is illegal beyond €3,000/month and leaves you with zero protection.

#6

Money transfer "for a friend"

Someone you barely know asks you to receive a payment in your bank account "because their card has issues" — and to forward it to a third party. This is money laundering. You are the criminal record.

→ Never let your bank account be used to receive and forward money for someone else, even a fellow Georgian. Belgian banks flag this within days.

#7

Fake police / tax office calls

"This is the Federal Police — pay this fine in vouchers immediately or you'll be deported." Real Belgian police never ask for payments by phone, never use crypto/vouchers, and never threaten deportation by phone.

→ Hang up. Call 101 (police) directly to verify. Real notices come by registered mail, never by phone or WhatsApp.

#8

Fake mutuality / utilities at the door

Someone in a uniform-looking jacket says they're from your mutuality / energy company and need your eID + bank card "to update your file." Sometimes followed by withdrawals from your account.

→ Never give your eID PIN or bank card to anyone at your door. Real institutions don't do door-to-door verification.

Red flags to watch for

სიფრთხილე! სიგნალები

  • Urgency. "You must decide today" or "transfer the deposit now." Scammers create artificial pressure.
  • დროის ზეწოლა. „გადაწყვიტე დღესვე" — თაღლითები ყოველთვის ხელოვნურ ზეწოლას ქმნიან.
  • Cash-only or crypto. Real Belgian institutions take SEPA bank transfers and provide written invoices.
  • Refuses to meet in person. "I'm out of the country, my friend will give you keys/contract."
  • Contract you can't read. Always have it translated before signing. A sworn translator costs ~€30–80 and saves €1000s.
  • Asks for full ID copies upfront. Especially for jobs that haven't been confirmed in writing.
  • "I'm Georgian too, trust me." Same nationality is not a guarantee. Verify everyone.
  • Deal too good to be true. Brussels apartment for €450/month? Job paying €30/hour with no qualifications? Scam.

If you've been scammed

თუ უკვე მოგატყუეს

  1. File a police report at your local police station. Bring all evidence — bank transfers, messages, contracts. Belgian police take fraud reports seriously and a record is needed for any insurance claim.
  2. Contact your bank immediately if money was transferred. Some transfers can be reversed within 24–48 hours.
  3. Report to the FPS Economy at meldpunt.belgie.be — Belgium's official scam reporting portal. Free, multilingual.
  4. If your eID was stolen, call 116 116 immediately to block it.
  5. Tell the community. Submit a report below so we can warn others about the pattern (we never publish names without legal review).
