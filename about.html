About

A community resource by and for Georgians living in or moving to Belgium.

Why this exists

Most of what a Georgian newcomer to Belgium needs to know — how to register, how renting works, where to find a doctor — is scattered across Facebook threads, half-translated government PDFs, and word-of-mouth. geosin.be collects answers to the most common questions in one place, in Georgian and English.

Eventually it will also include a directory of community-run services (drivers, plumbers, language teachers, etc.) and a discussion forum.

Community resources

Beyond this site, here's where the Georgian community in Belgium gathers:

  • Facebook group "Georgians in Belgium" — thousands of members, very active for day-to-day questions.
  • Embassy of Georgia in Brussels — official events, document services, formal community gatherings.
  • Georgian Orthodox Church — services in several Belgian cities.

How submissions and data work

The Services page lets community members register their services — drivers, plumbers, teachers, etc. Submissions are reviewed before publication. Once approved, the public listing shows the contact details the submitter provided, exactly so others can reach out. Submissions that are spam, off-topic, or unverifiable are not published. If you submitted something and want it removed, send an email and it'll be deleted.

Contact

For questions, corrections, additions, or removal requests: open an issue on the project repository on GitHub, or reach out via the Facebook group above.

Open source

The site's code is public on GitHub. Contributions, suggestions, and translations are welcome. This is also the author's first software project — be kind.
